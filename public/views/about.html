CrossFit Analytics is an initiative to combine data with CrossFit training. By analyzing and exposing the scores of the best CrossFit athletes, the idea is that patterns will begin to emerge that can highlight exactly what it takes to be great in the sport of fitness.



How are these numbers generated and are they accurate?

All numbers have been taken from the athlete bios on games.crossfit.com. The "Top 10" category only analyzes the scores of the top 10 Games finishers (separated by gender) from the selected year. The "Games" category analyzes the scores of all the Games competitors from the selected year. The "Regionals" category analyzes the scores of the top 15 Regional competitors from each region for that year, and the "Open" category analyzes the scores of the top 600 Open finishers worldwide for that year.
At present, only the average scores of these groups are displayed. Some athletes did not provide information for all of the fields (i.e. they may not have provided a "Helen" time or a max clean and jerk). In those cases, the algorithm simply skipped over that field for that athlete and continued on with the others. There was a lot of data, so the numbers are still highly accurate even though not all athletes completed their bios.
